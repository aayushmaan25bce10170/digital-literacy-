
CASE STUDY: UPI / ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD
UPI fraud is one of the most common forms of cybercrime in India, where fraudsters trick individuals into transferring money or sharing sensitive information. This type of fraud typically occurs through fake payment requests, phishing links, or impersonation.

In a common scenario, a scammer poses as a buyer or a bank representative and contacts the victim. The fraudster may send a QR code or a payment request and instruct the victim to approve it in order to receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment, resulting in a loss of funds. In other cases, victims are asked to share OTPs or click on malicious links, which leads to unauthorized access to their bank accounts.

Students and young adults are often targeted due to frequent use of digital payment platforms and lack of awareness about such scams. The consequences include financial loss, data theft, and emotional stress.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious and well-informed while using online payment systems.
